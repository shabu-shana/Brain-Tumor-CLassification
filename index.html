Understanding MRI Scans

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a non-invasive medical imaging technique that produces detailed images of organs and tissues in the body. It uses a strong magnetic field and radio waves to generate images, making it an invaluable tool for visualizing the brain.

This technology helps doctors detect and monitor a wide range of conditions, including tumors. Our application uses a similar process to analyze images, but for educational and conceptual purposes only.

The Deep Learning Model

The analyzer uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) deep learning model, which is a type of neural network. This model was trained on a large dataset of brain MRI scans to recognize patterns associated with different types of tumors: Meningioma, Glioma, Pituitary, or No Tumor.

When you upload an image, the model analyzes it instantly on your device to provide a conceptual classification. This process is very fast and respects your privacy, as no data is sent to a server.
